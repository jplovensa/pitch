The SECHA Engine

Renovation is a standardized credit trigger. The engine converts unknown physical risk into measurable creditworthiness.

  • 1) Risk Scoring: structured condition assessment → risk profile
  • 2) Scope Standardization: fixed intervention bands → reduced variance
  • 3) Decisioning: rules-based underwriting compatible with lenders
  • 4) Orchestration: milestone disbursement + monitoring + reporting
  • 5) Feedback Loop: outcomes improve eligibility + pricing

Scale rule

If it doesn’t fit a SKU, it doesn’t scale. This prevents SECHA becoming a bespoke services business.

The Problem

Credit can’t see inside the asset.

  • Condition risk is opaque → underwriting fails
  • Renovation requires upfront cash → demand stalls
  • Mortgage top-ups are slow & rigid
  • Paylater is not built for housing

System outcome

  • Trapped value in secondary homes
  • Idle bank liquidity with limited safe deployment
  • Transactions don’t move; communities don’t upgrade
